Wired vs Wireless

When data is transmitted on a network, it can do so wired or wirelessly.

Obviously, wired transmission refers to when data is transferred through a cable. USually it's an electrical signal (e.g. in a USB or HDMI cable), or an optical cable using light (usually for audio).

Wireless uses radio waves / microwaves / infrared waves to transmit / recieve data

Types of Cabels

Wired cable - copper cables come in three types - a coaxial cable, used for audio, which is a single wire that is surrounded by insulation, an unshielded/shielded twisted pair, which are pairs of wires that are insulated / not insulated within. Insulated is better as it prevents EMF interference which causes static.

Copper cables are cheap, but cannot go very far. They have medium/moderate transfer speeds.

Fibre optic cables - they use thin strands of glass, that reflect light signals within them. Each individual cable can only send data in one direction (unlike insulated copper wires within a bigger cable), so two cables are needed for duplex. Expensive, but very fast and no signal interference

Wireless

WiFi uses radio waves. DAta is transmitted across a range of frequencies, depending on traffic and preventing clashses / interference. Signals weaken over distance, so is limited by range, especially metal and concrete, it cannot pass thru. Slower than wired connections.

Satellite - a satphone can communicate directly to any other phone in the world, regardless of infrastructure and existance of internet cables. Extremely expensive, and slower.

Infrared - emits IR light, very short range, must be directly in line of sight, does not work if blocked by any object. Used for TV remotes.

RFID - uses radio waves to trasmit data to read/write info from tags. Tag doesn't need power, and is cheap. Passive RFID reads, Acive RFID writes data. USed for things like self checkouts, library books, access cards

Bluetooth - a very low power radio wave, connects directly to the device. Small range. very power efficient. For things like speakers, keyboards, mice, headphones
